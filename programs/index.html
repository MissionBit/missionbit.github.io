Fall 2015

High School Classes

High school students from any SFUSD or charter high school are welcome to take Mission Bit classes free of charge. The courses below for fall semester 2015 high school classes are hosted at Mission High School.
Students that successfully complete their course at Mission Bit will have the option of opting out of the comparable CCSF course. More details will be provided at our accepted student orientation.
Students that complete a semester with Mission Bit will be eligible for an opportunity to attend Hack Reactor tuition free.
All 2015 fall semester students will participate in class visit a SF Bay Area tech companies.
All 2015 fall high school classes go from September 14th 2015 and end Dec 10th 2015.
Mandatory: All students/families will need to attend accepted student orientation Saturday September 12th 2015 from 3pm to 5pm at 699 Townsend. More details provided upon acceptance.
Demo Day: Each semesters end with Demo Day, which is a mandatory component of classes participation.

For additional questions, you can contact Mission Bit CEO Stevon Cook at stevon@missionbit.com.

Intro Web Programming

This is our beginners course. In the Intro Web Programming class, students will learn HTML5, CSS3 and Javascript and some core programming concepts through project based instruction. Within the semester the class will build websites and interactive applications. At the end of the semester, the class is broken up into teams, each paired with an instructor and spends a few weeks building a final project.

Meets: Tuesday and Thursday, 4pm - 6pm. The first class is Tuesday September 15th 2015.

Location:  Mission High School
